# Product & Business Unknown Unknowns Checklist

This dimension is NOT about judging whether an idea is good or bad. It's about surfacing the business and product blind spots that developers — especially technically-minded ones — consistently miss. The focus is on factual gaps, not opinions.

## Competition & Precedents
- Who has tried this before? What happened? (Failure reasons are gold — they reveal the real challenges)
- Who is doing something similar right now, even if not identical?
- What's the incumbent solution people use today, even if it's hacky? (Spreadsheets, WhatsApp groups, and manual processes are competitors too)
- Why might the existing solutions be "good enough" for most people?
- Is there a reason this hasn't been built yet? (Sometimes the answer is "the market doesn't want it" — sometimes it's "the timing wasn't right until now")
- Open source alternatives that could undercut a paid product

## Distribution & Growth
- "If you build it, they will come" is almost never true — what's the actual distribution channel?
- Paid acquisition costs in the target market (CAC) vs lifetime value (LTV) — has this been thought about at all?
- Is there a natural viral loop, or does every user need to be acquired individually?
- Community-driven growth requires community-building skills, not just code
- SEO takes 6-12 months to compound — what happens in the meantime?
- Social media as a distribution channel requires consistent content, not just a launch post
- Partnership and integration channels (being where users already are)
- Developer tools need developer advocacy, not traditional marketing

## Cold Start Problem (Platforms & Marketplaces)
- Two-sided platforms (buyers/sellers, makers/voters, creators/consumers) need both sides simultaneously
- Which side do you attract first? The harder side usually needs to come first
- Can you fake or seed the supply side early? (Curate content manually, be the first "maker," etc.)
- Single-player mode — does the product provide value even with zero other users?
- Geographic cold start — a marketplace that needs local density (like Uber) has a different challenge than one that's global from day one
- Chicken-and-egg problems in network effects

## Business Model
- Who pays and why? Is the value proposition clear enough that someone would pay?
- Freemium conversion rates are typically 2-5% — does the math work at that rate?
- If ad-supported: the traffic volumes needed to generate meaningful revenue are enormous
- Pricing in LATAM markets requires localized pricing (purchasing power parity)
- Transaction-based models need high volume or high ticket — which one?
- If the product is free, what's the moat? (Free products with no moat get cloned fast)
- Revenue timing — how long until the first dollar? Can you sustain until then?
- Are the incentives aligned? (Does what's good for you align with what's good for your users?)

## Market & Timing
- Is this a "vitamin" (nice to have) or a "painkiller" (must have)? Painkillers are much easier to sell
- Is the market growing, stable, or shrinking?
- Regulatory tailwinds or headwinds that could change the landscape
- Platform dependency — if your product depends on another platform's API/ecosystem, they can cut you off or compete with you
- Economic conditions in target market affecting willingness to pay
- Seasonal or cyclical demand patterns

## User Behavior & Retention
- Acquisition is easy compared to retention — what brings users back?
- How often does a user need this product? Daily? Weekly? Once? (Low frequency = hard retention)
- What's the switching cost? If it's zero, users leave easily
- The "first 5 minutes" experience determines if a user stays — is there an onboarding plan?
- Power users vs casual users — who matters more for the business?
- Feedback loops — how do you learn what users actually want vs what you think they want?

## Execution Risks
- Solo founder building everything = burnout risk and bus factor of 1
- Trying to build for "everyone" usually means building for no one — who is the specific first user?
- Feature creep before product-market fit
- Premature optimization (building for scale before validating demand)
- Building in isolation without talking to potential users
- Confusing "people say they'd use this" with "people actually use this"

## Common by Product Type

### Marketplace / Platform
- Commission rates need to be high enough to sustain the business but low enough that users don't go around you (disintermediation)
- Trust and safety infrastructure (fraud, disputes, fake listings)
- Payment escrow for transactions between users
- Review/rating systems and their manipulation
- Liquidity — enough supply and demand to match consistently

### SaaS / B2B
- Enterprise sales cycles are 3-12 months — can you survive?
- Compliance requirements from buyers (SOC 2, ISO 27001) are expensive
- Integration requirements with existing tools
- Multi-tenant architecture decisions affect everything
- Contract and billing complexity (annual vs monthly, seats vs usage)

### Developer Tools
- Developers are ruthless evaluators — if the DX is bad, they leave in minutes
- Documentation IS the product for developer tools
- Open source expectations — developers often expect tools to be open source
- Community building is essential but time-consuming
- Competing with free/open-source alternatives

### Consumer Social / Community
- Network effects take time — the product feels empty/broken until critical mass
- Content moderation at scale is a full-time job (or multiple)
- Toxicity management — one bad actor can poison a community
- Engagement metrics can be misleading (vanity metrics vs real value)
- Cultural differences across regions affect product-market fit
