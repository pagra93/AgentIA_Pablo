# Legal & Compliance Unknown Unknowns Checklist

## Data Privacy Regulations
- GDPR applies if you have ANY users in the EU (not just EU-based companies)
- CCPA/CPRA applies if you handle California residents' data (even from abroad)
- Ley de Protección de Datos Personales (Argentina) — requires consent, data access rights, registration with AAIP
- Brazil's LGPD — similar to GDPR, applies to Brazilian users
- Data residency requirements — some countries require data stored locally
- Right to deletion — can you actually delete a user's data from all systems including backups?
- Data portability — users can request their data in machine-readable format
- Cookie consent banners legally required in EU (not just best practice)
- Privacy policy required by law in most jurisdictions
- Data breach notification requirements (72 hours in GDPR)
- Data Processing Agreements (DPA) required with all third-party processors
- Children's data (COPPA in US, similar laws globally) — extra protections under 13/16

## Terms of Service
- Without ToS, you have no legal framework for user disputes
- Limitation of liability clauses
- Acceptable use policies
- Account termination rights
- Intellectual property ownership of user-generated content
- Dispute resolution / arbitration clauses
- Jurisdiction and governing law

## Industry-Specific Compliance

### Healthcare
- HIPAA (US) — applies to anyone handling Protected Health Information
- Ley de Telemedicina / regulations by jurisdiction in LATAM
- Medical device regulations if software provides diagnosis/treatment recommendations
- Prescription handling regulations
- Patient data retention requirements (often 7+ years)

### Finance / Payments
- PCI DSS for any credit card data handling
- KYC (Know Your Customer) requirements
- AML (Anti Money Laundering) regulations
- SOX compliance for financial reporting
- Open Banking regulations (PSD2 in EU)
- Cryptocurrency regulations (varies wildly by country)
- BCRA regulations for financial services in Argentina

### Education
- FERPA (US) for student data
- Age-appropriate design codes
- Accessibility requirements (often legally mandated)

### E-commerce
- Consumer protection laws (right of withdrawal, refund policies)
- Tax collection obligations (sales tax, VAT, IVA)
- Cross-border commerce regulations
- Product liability
- Defensa del Consumidor (Argentina) — strong consumer protection

## Intellectual Property
- Using open-source code with incompatible licenses (GPL in proprietary software)
- AI-generated content ownership is legally unclear
- Font licensing — many "free" fonts aren't free for commercial use
- Stock photo licensing violations
- Trademark infringement in app/product naming
- Patent risks (especially in US, software patents are broad)
- Scraping data may violate ToS of source sites

## Accessibility
- ADA (US) — websites are considered "places of public accommodation"
- EAA (European Accessibility Act) — effective 2025
- WCAG 2.1 AA is the de facto legal standard
- Lawsuits for inaccessible websites are increasing rapidly
- Government contracts often require Section 508 compliance

## Employment & Contractor
- Misclassification of contractors vs employees
- IP assignment agreements with contractors
- Non-compete and non-solicitation enforceability varies by jurisdiction
- International contractor compliance (permanent establishment risk)

## AI-Specific Legal
- EU AI Act — risk-based regulation, some AI uses are banned
- Disclosure requirements — telling users they're interacting with AI
- Liability for AI-generated outputs (misinformation, harmful advice)
- Training data copyright issues
- Bias and discrimination in AI decisions (especially hiring, lending, housing)
- Right to explanation for automated decisions (GDPR Article 22)
